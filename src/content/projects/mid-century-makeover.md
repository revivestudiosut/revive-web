The living room makes the case for working with what a house already has. An ornate antique fireplace surround — all carved scrollwork and heavy molding — got a coat of deep slate-green paint that turns a dated relic into the room's sculptural anchor. We flanked it with custom walnut built-ins and hung a long, panoramic desert landscape painting above the mantel, then tucked a turntable, a stack of vinyl, and a few well-worn paperbacks into the shelves so the room reads collected rather than staged.

The baths carry the mid-century vocabulary in a richer key. Floating walnut vanities sit against linear tile — inky navy in the powder room, soft blue-gray subway in the primary — paired with unlacquered-brass wall-mounted faucets and slim cross handles that patina beautifully over time. An organic pebble-shaped mirror and a pair of oxblood-red sconces keep the powder room playful; the primary bath answers with rounded brass-framed mirrors, a double vanity, and a walnut-look shower wrapped in vertical tile with a bronze rain head and a penny-tile niche.

The personality lives in the details. A small framed sketch — "a memory of food" — hangs in the tiled water closet, an agave in a simple ceramic pot catches the light off the navy tile, and the mudroom-laundry keeps the story going with walnut cabinetry, sage-green tile, and a stacked LG washer and dryer. It's a home that respects its era without turning into a museum of it.
