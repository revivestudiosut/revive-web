The kitchen sets the tone for the whole house. Crisp white shaker cabinetry runs to the ceiling on either side of a black plaster range hood, framed by a pair of antique-brass lantern pendants and a soft gray glazed subway tile. We grounded all that brightness with a warm white-oak island and black cane-back stools, then let a professional spring faucet and an integrated prep sink do the working-kitchen heavy lifting. A walk-in pantry just off the room carries the oak through on the lowers with a honed dark counter, so the everyday small appliances have a home out of sight.

Past the kitchen, the great room leans into contrast — a floor-to-ceiling stacked-stone fireplace, a black grand piano, and a single linen lounge chair, kept deliberately spare so the materials carry the space. Up the stairs, a navy spindle console anchors the landing beneath a pair of soft, atmospheric landscape canvases, with a fern, white roses, and a little white horse to keep it from feeling too serious.

The bath and laundry are where the house quietly shows off. The primary bath pairs a freestanding tub and aged-brass tub filler with an oak double vanity, black-framed mirrors, and a patterned marble floor; the adjacent shower stacks soft vertical tile around a penny-tile bench, with watercolor protea prints for a botanical pause. Next door, the sage-green laundry — deep undermount sink, black coil faucet, stacked Miele machines, and mountain views out the window — gets the same hardware and the same care as the rooms guests actually see.
