The kitchen update is all about warmth and quality. Rift-cut white-oak cabinetry in a soft greige stain wraps the room — including a custom hood that reads as built-in millwork rather than an appliance — and glass-front uppers give a few favorite objects a lit display. A glossy, handmade-look marble subway backsplash catches the light against quartzite countertops, and a professional Wolf range anchors the cooking wall. The island, topped in the same marbled stone, adds a quiet navy accent on its base so the oak has something to play against.

The primary bath leans into a single material done well: Carrara marble, top to bottom. The walk-in shower pairs large-format marble walls with a hex-tile floor and a built-in bench, while the freestanding slipper tub sits under the window with a brushed-gold floor-mounted filler and a herringbone marble floor underfoot. It's a restrained, classic palette — the kind of refresh that will still look right in twenty years.
