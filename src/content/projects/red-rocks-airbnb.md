A short-term rental has to do two things at once: photograph beautifully and survive a rotating cast of guests. We leaned into both with an open great room built around a soft sage-green island, two oversized woven rattan dome pendants, and a pair of durable gray sofas that shrug off real use. Terracotta drapery and a warm wood-look floor pull the desert palette indoors, while a framed canvas of a lone desert tree gives the seating area a focal point without anything precious to break.

The entry gallery wall is the room guests remember. We layered framed prints of a saguaro, Monument Valley, and a Joshua Tree road with a hexagon wall planter, terracotta vessels, a potted aloe, and — the quiet favorite — a tiny mint-green VW bus. It's southwestern without tipping into cliché: enough story to place you firmly in red-rock country the moment you walk in, and styled to look just as good on a listing photo as it does in person.
